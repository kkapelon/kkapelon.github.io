I like:

  • Clean abstractions and API walls

  • The Unix philosophy that each program should do one thing well
  • Build pipelines where everything is automated

I don't like

  • Over-Engineering and Lasagna code
  • XML Gluttony
  • Monolithic systems
  • Design Pattern overload
  • Projects that fall under Conway's law or suffer from the Inner Platform effect

I am not interested in joining your company if:

  • you get less than 6/12 points in the Joel Test.
  • your recruitment process involves trivia/quiz questions that have nothing to do with software engineering
  • your software architect thinks that design patterns are the next best thing since sliced bread
  • you want to rewrite your big application from PHP to Java (thanks but no thanks)
